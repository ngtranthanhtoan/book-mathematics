# Appendix I: Recommended Reading

> Annotated bibliography organized by topic. These are the books and resources that complement this one -- use them to dive deeper into whatever grabs you.

---

## Foundational Mathematics

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *How to Solve It* | George Polya | Mathematical thinking and problem-solving strategies. The origin of many ideas in Level 0. |
| *Book of Proof* | Richard Hammack | Logic, sets, and proof techniques. Free online. Good companion for Levels 0-1. |
| *Mathematics for Computer Science* | Lehman, Leighton, Meyer | Discrete math for programmers. MIT OpenCourseWare. Covers graphs, probability, and proof. |

---

## Linear Algebra

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Linear Algebra Done Right* | Sheldon Axler | Conceptual understanding without determinant-heavy proofs. Theory-focused complement to Level 4. |
| *Introduction to Linear Algebra* | Gilbert Strang | Practical, computational approach. Closest in spirit to how Level 4 teaches. |
| *Coding the Matrix* | Philip Klein | Linear algebra through Python programming. Code-first like this book. |
| *Linear Algebra and Its Applications* | David Lay | Standard undergraduate text. Good for exercises. |

---

## Calculus

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Calculus* | Michael Spivak | Rigorous foundations. If you want proofs behind the formulas in Level 6. |
| *Calculus Made Easy* | Silvanus Thompson | Intuitive, beginner-friendly. Good warmup before Level 6. |
| *Active Calculus* | Matt Boelkins | Free, activity-based learning. Hands-on approach. |

---

## Probability & Statistics

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Introduction to Probability* | Blitzstein & Hwang | Intuitive probability with real examples. Harvard course companion. Deepens Levels 7-8. |
| *Think Stats* | Allen Downey | Statistics for programmers. Free online. Same audience as this book. |
| *All of Statistics* | Larry Wasserman | Concise graduate-level reference. Fast-paced but thorough. |
| *Statistical Inference* | Casella & Berger | Rigorous statistical theory. For when you want the full proofs behind Level 8. |

---

## Machine Learning Mathematics

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Mathematics for Machine Learning* | Deisenroth, Faisal, Ong | **Best overall ML math book.** Free PDF. Covers similar ground to this book but with a more traditional approach. |
| *The Matrix Calculus You Need for Deep Learning* | Parr & Howard | Derivatives and gradients for neural networks. Free. Perfect companion for Levels 6 and 13. |
| *Deep Learning* | Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville | Math foundations in Part I. Free online. The standard deep learning textbook. |
| *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning* | Christopher Bishop | Probabilistic ML, Bayesian methods. Graduate level. Deepens Levels 7-9 and 13. |

---

## Optimization

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Convex Optimization* | Boyd & Vandenberghe | Definitive optimization text. Free PDF. The full theory behind Level 9. |
| *Numerical Optimization* | Nocedal & Wright | Practical optimization algorithms. Complements Levels 9 and 12. |

---

## Information Theory

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Elements of Information Theory* | Cover & Thomas | Standard reference for everything in Level 10 and beyond. |
| *Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms* | David MacKay | ML perspective on information theory. Free online. Bridges Levels 7, 10, and 13. |

---

## Advanced Topics

| Book | Author | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Analysis I & II* | Terence Tao | Rigorous real analysis. Foundations for Level 14 topics. |
| *Topology* | James Munkres | Foundational topology. For the topology section of Level 14. |
| *Stochastic Processes* | Sheldon Ross | Random processes for ML. Diffusion models, Markov chains. |

## Suggested Learning Paths (External Resources)

These are paths through the *recommended reading* above, not through this book.

**Minimal ML Path** (fastest route):
1. *Mathematics for Machine Learning* (Deisenroth) -- covers essentials
2. *Deep Learning* Part I (Goodfellow) -- fills gaps

**Rigorous Path** (deep understanding):
1. *Book of Proof* (Hammack) -- foundations
2. *Linear Algebra Done Right* (Axler) -- theory
3. *Introduction to Probability* (Blitzstein) -- probability
4. *Convex Optimization* (Boyd) -- optimization
5. *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning* (Bishop) -- synthesis

**Programmer's Path** (code-first):
1. *Coding the Matrix* (Klein) -- linear algebra
2. *Think Stats* (Downey) -- statistics
3. *Mathematics for Machine Learning* (Deisenroth) -- ML math
